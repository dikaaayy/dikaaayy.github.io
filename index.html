The future of waste management in Indonesia

How bad is it

A University of Georgia study released in 2015 found that Indonesia's oceans are the second waters in the world to store the most plastic waste. This is not an achievement but an alarm that indicates that Indonesia is not only a land waste crisis, but has also penetrated into its waters.

Data from the Ministry of Environment and Forestry (KLHK) in February 2019, released that currently Indonesia produces at least 64 million tons of waste piles every year. Based on these data, about 60 percent of the waste is transported and dumped to the landfill, 10 percent of the waste is recycled, while the other 30 percent is not managed and pollutes the environment.

Is it concerning?

Researchers can project how effective intervention measures, such as increasing recycling, reducing production, and replacing plastics with other materials will be. However, despite all that we do, according to Dr Velis, his study projects that there will be an additional 710 million tonnes of plastic waste polluting the environment by 2040.

There is no easy solution to the plastic problem. However, a topic that is sometimes overlooked but highlighted by this study is the fact that some 2 billion people in the southern part of the world do not have access to proper waste treatment.

Act now!

Gardening at home can be done in various ways. You don't have to worry if you don't have a large area of land, because we can use used bottles as planting media. Pots from used bottles can also look artistic. So that it can be used to grow flowers and vegetables, without having to buy a new pot.

Using plastic straws has become a common necessity. So to change that habit is difficult, but not impossible. If you have to use straws, use stainless steel straws that can be washed and reused or paper straws.

In most large shopping centers, regulations regarding paid plastic bags have been enacted, but not a few are willing to spend money to keep using plastic bags. Look for shopping bags of various sizes so that they can be adjusted to your shopping. That way you will greatly reduce the use of plastic bags.

After just one single use, most of them are thrown out and end up in landfills and in our waterways. Plastic cutlery is one of those items that won’t get recycled even when you put it in the recycling. It’s too contaminated. It’s too small. It's too lightweight. So those 40 billion plastic utensils per year are a complete waste.
